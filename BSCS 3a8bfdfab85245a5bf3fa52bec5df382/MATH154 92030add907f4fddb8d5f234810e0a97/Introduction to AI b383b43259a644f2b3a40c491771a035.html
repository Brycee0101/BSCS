Chinese Room Argument (1980)

a thought-experiment published by American philosopher John Searle, where he imagines himself alone in a room following a computer program for responding to Chinese characters slipped under the door.

  • Person = CPU
  • Rule Book = AI Program
  • Paper = Storage

Searle argues that the room has no intelligence in it, it has no understanding of what they mean but gives perfectly reasonable answers in Chinese!

Rational Behavior = doing the right thing which is expected to maximize goal achievement, given the available information.

This does not necessarily involve thinking but thinking should be in the service of rational action
