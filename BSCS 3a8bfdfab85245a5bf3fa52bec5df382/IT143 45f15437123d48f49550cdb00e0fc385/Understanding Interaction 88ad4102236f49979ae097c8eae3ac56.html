Understanding Interaction

Human Computer Interaction (HCI)

  • about extending and supporting all manner of human activities in all manner of places.
  • facilitating user experience (UX) through designing interactions.
  • makes work effective, efficient and safer.
  • Improves and enhances learning and training
  • provides enjoyable and exciting entertainment
  • enhances communication and understanding
  • supports new forms of creativity and expression.

Problem Space

having a good understanding of the problem space can help inform the design space.

Framework

  1. Are there problems with an existing product?
  1. Why do you think there are problems?
  1. Why do you think your proposed ideas might be useful?
  1. How would you see people using it with their current way of doing things?
  1. How will it support people in their activities?
  1. Will it really help them?

Design Space

Examples

  1. What kind of interface?
  1. What functionality it provides?
  1. How does it behave?

Conceptual Model

the proposed system in terms of a set of integrated ideas and concepts about what it should do, behave and look like, that will be understandable by the users in the manner intended.

  • thinking how the system will appear to users, and how they will understand it.

Questions to Consider in Formulating a Conceptual Model

  • What will the users be doing when carrying out their tasks?
  • What kind of interface metaphor, if any, will be appropriate?
  • How will the system support these?

  • What kind of interaction modes and styles to use?

    ❗Always keep in mind how the user will understand the underlying conceptual model.
